# 5-minute Loom talk track

## 0:00-0:30 - Opening
Hi, my name is Oksana, and today I will briefly present my solution for the TaskFlow case.

In this presentation, I will explain the core reasons behind TaskFlow's decline, review the market and competitor landscape, and present the strategy I would recommend for the product over the next 12 months.

## 0:30-1:30 - Diagnosis
My main conclusion is that TaskFlow's core issue is not a shrinking market. The real issue is weakening product-market fit in its best audience and best monetizing markets.

The strongest signal is retention deterioration. D30 retention fell from 12% in Q1 2024 to 5% in Q4 2025, while D90 retention fell from 6.5% to 2.3%. Paid churn also increased from 10% to 18%.

At the same time, the product has not had a major feature release in eight months, store ratings fell from 4.6 to 4.2, and user complaints are concentrated around limited collaboration, no offline mode, and a repetitive UI.

## 1:30-2:10 - Why acquisition is not the fix
Acquisition became more expensive and less efficient. CPI increased from $1.8 to $2.6, 62% of installs now come from Meta, and organic share is declining.

Using the provided numbers, approximate paid CAC is around $26.5, while approximate paid LTV is only about $26.7. This means scaling paid acquisition right now would be risky.

## 2:10-3:00 - Market and competition
The category is still attractive. Public market sources estimate task management software is growing at a low-teens CAGR, and broader non-game mobile app spending reached about $85 billion in 2025.

So I would not recommend abandoning productivity. However, leaders like Todoist, TickTick, Any.do, and Microsoft To Do are better positioned. They ship more frequently, offer deeper collaboration, stronger calendar workflows, richer templates, and better distribution through SEO, integrations, or ecosystem bundling.

## 3:00-4:15 - Recommendation
My recommendation is a turnaround strategy, not a full pivot. TaskFlow should reposition itself as a calendar-first planner for young professionals, freelancers, and duos.

There are five parts to the strategy:
1. Fix the core workflow with offline mode, better onboarding, a UI refresh, improved recurring tasks, and stronger calendar parity.
2. Expand collaboration from 3 to 5 users with comments and attachments.
3. Keep pricing broadly stable, but relax free limits and test a 14-day trial and annual-first packaging.
4. Cut inefficient paid UA and shift effort toward ASO, templates, referrals, and creator partnerships.
5. Prioritize high-value English-speaking markets first: the US, UK, Canada, and Australia.

## 4:15-5:00 - Targets and close
If execution is strong, I would target D30 retention of 8% to 9%, paid churn of 11% to 12%, organic share of installs at 25%, and MRR recovery to roughly $78k to $85k over 12 months.

So overall, my conclusion is simple: TaskFlow should stay in the productivity category, avoid a risky full pivot for now, and focus on restoring stronger product value, sharper positioning, and more efficient growth.
